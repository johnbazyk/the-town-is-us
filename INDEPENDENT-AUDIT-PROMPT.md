# Independent Audit Prompt

**Copy and paste the prompt below into ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any AI tool you trust. It will walk you through an independent review of the Granby Accountability Dashboard.**

You don't need any technical skills. The AI will check the site's claims against publicly available sources and tell you what it finds — including anything that looks wrong, misleading, or biased.

---

## The Prompt

```
I want you to perform an independent, unbiased audit of a civic data website about Granby, Connecticut. Your job is to evaluate whether the site presents data accurately, whether the sources are real and verifiable, and whether the presentation is fair or biased in any direction.

Do not assume the site is accurate. Do not assume it is inaccurate. Evaluate it on its merits.

Here are the resources to review:

1. THE WEBSITE: https://thetownisus.netlify.app
2. THE SOURCE CODE: https://github.com/johnbazyk/the-town-is-us
3. THE BUILD PROCESS: https://github.com/johnbazyk/the-town-is-us/blob/main/SITE-UPDATE-SPEC.md
4. THE QA AUDIT: https://github.com/johnbazyk/the-town-is-us/blob/main/qa-reports/QA-AUDIT-2026-04-04.md
5. THE METHODOLOGY WRITEUP: https://github.com/johnbazyk/the-town-is-us/blob/main/HOW-THIS-SITE-WAS-BUILT.md

Please evaluate the following and provide your findings for each:

SECTION A — SOURCE VERIFICATION
For at least 5 specific data claims on the site, try to verify them against the cited sources or other publicly available records. Check:
- Are the source citations real documents that exist?
- Do the numbers on the site match what the sources actually say?
- Are any numbers taken out of context or presented in a misleading way?

SECTION B — BIAS ANALYSIS
Evaluate the overall presentation for bias:
- Does the site present data selectively (e.g., showing only unfavorable trends while omitting favorable ones)?
- Is the language neutral and factual, or does it use emotionally charged framing?
- Are there important counterarguments, context, or alternative explanations that the site leaves out?
- Does the site give fair treatment to all parties mentioned (school board, superintendent, teachers, taxpayers)?
- Are the comparisons to peer districts (Simsbury, Avon, Farmington, Suffield) fair and methodologically sound?

SECTION C — METHODOLOGY REVIEW
Review how the site was built:
- Is the AI disclosure adequate? Does it clearly explain what AI did and didn't do?
- Is the data verification process (OCR of scanned PDFs, cross-confirmation) credible?
- Are estimated figures properly identified and disclosed?
- Is the source code consistent with the claims made about the build process?

SECTION D — FACT-CHECK TAB REVIEW
The site includes a "Fact-Check" tab that evaluates claims from local newspaper articles. Review this section specifically:
- Are the original claims represented fairly, or are they strawmanned?
- Are the rebuttals supported by the cited data?
- Are the verdict labels (misleading, false, missing context, true) justified?

SECTION E — OVERALL ASSESSMENT
Provide an overall assessment:
- On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate the site's accuracy?
- On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate the site's fairness/balance?
- What are the strongest aspects of the site?
- What are the weakest aspects or biggest concerns?
- What specific improvements would make the site more credible or balanced?

Be thorough and honest. If the site is accurate, say so. If it's biased, say so. If it's a mix, explain where it succeeds and where it falls short.
```

---

## Why This Matters

This site was built by a Granby taxpayer and it makes claims about public spending, academic outcomes, and school governance. You should not have to take the author's word for any of it.

The prompt above gives any AI tool — including ones the author has no control over — the information it needs to check the work independently. If the data is wrong, the audit will find it. If the framing is unfair, the audit will flag it.

If you run this audit and find errors, there's a submission form on the site to report them. Corrections are welcome.
